Umami Burger with Port and Stilton (30 min)

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped shallots
  • 1/2 cup port wine
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 4 ounces Stilton cheese, crumbled
  • 1 1/2 pounds ground beef chuck, preferably 85 percent lean
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon umami seasoning
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Kosher salt
  • 4 brioche buns, split and toasted
  • 2 cups baby arugula

Directions

  • In a small saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the shallots and cook, stirring occasionally, until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the port and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat and simmer until reduced by half, about 5 minutes. Stir in the heavy cream and cook until slightly thickened, about 2 minutes. Remove from the heat and stir in the Stilton until melted. Keep warm.
  • In a large bowl, combine the ground beef with the onion powder, garlic powder, umami seasoning, Worcestershire sauce, 1 teaspoon of kosher salt, and freshly ground black pepper. Knead gently until thoroughly mixed. Form the beef into four 4-inch patties.
  • Heat a grill pan or griddle over medium-high heat. Season the patties with salt and pepper. Cook the burgers until browned on the bottom, about 3 minutes. Flip the burgers and cook until the desired doneness, about 3 minutes longer for medium.
  • Mound the arugula on the bun bottoms and top with the burgers. Spoon the Stilton sauce over the burgers. Top with the bun tops and serve immediately.

Make Ahead

The Stilton sauce can be made up to 1 day ahead and refrigerated. Reheat gently before serving.
